# Mineral Species — Descloizite

## Species: Descloizite

### Identity
- **Formula:** PbZn(VO₄)(OH)
- **Crystal system:** Orthorhombic (dipyramidal, space group Pnma)
- **Mineral group:** Vanadate (adelite-descloizite group)
- **Hardness (Mohs):** 3–3.5
- **Specific gravity:** 6.1–6.2 (very heavy for a non-sulfide — lead-dominated)
- **Cleavage:** None
- **Fracture:** Irregular, sub-conchoidal
- **Luster:** Greasy to vitreous

### Color & Appearance
- **Typical color:** Deep cherry-red, reddish brown, brownish red, nearly black
- **Color causes:** V⁴⁺/V⁵⁺ charge transfer, Fe³⁺ substitution; darker with Fe content
- **Transparency:** Transparent to opaque
- **Streak:** Orange-yellow to brownish red
- **Notable visual features:** Very high refractive indices (2.185–2.350), strong birefringence (0.165), greasy luster unlike most vanadates

### Crystal Habits
- **Primary habit:** Zoned tabular crystals, spear-point bladed prisms
- **Common forms/faces:** Prismatic with terminal pyramids; often tabular on {010}
- **Twin laws:** Not commonly twinned
- **Varieties:** Cuprodescloizite (Cu-rich intermediate, greenish, historically a separate name)
- **Special morphologies:** Drusy crusts, stalactitic aggregates, fibrous encrusting masses with mammillary surface, plumose aggregates

### Formation Conditions (SIMULATOR PARAMETERS)

#### Temperature
- **Nucleation temperature range:** Near-ambient to ~80°C (supergene/oxidation zone)
- **Optimal growth temperature:** 20–50°C
- **Decomposition temperature:** ~600–700°C (decomposes to lead vanadate glass + ZnO)
- **Temperature-dependent habits:** Higher temperatures favor more crystalline/tabular forms; low T produces fibrous/botryoidal crusts

#### Chemistry Required
- **Required elements in broth:** Pb²⁺ (>100 ppm), Zn²⁺ (>50 ppm), V⁵⁰ as VO₄³⁻ (>10 ppm)
- **Optional/enhancing elements:** Cu²⁺ (forms mottramite series intermediate), Ga³⁺ (substitutes for Zn³⁺), Ge⁴⁺ (substitutes), As⁵⁺ (partial substitution for V⁵⁺ → arsendescloizite component)
- **Inhibiting elements:** Fe²⁺ (quenches growth, promotes competing minerals), high Ca²⁺ (sequesters V as descloizite doesn't incorporate Ca)
- **Required pH range:** 5–8 (neutral to mildly acidic; vanadate is mobile in this range)
- **Required Eh range:** Oxidizing (V must be V⁵⁺ as vanadate; Pb and Zn are already divalent)
- **Required O₂ range:** High — requires oxidizing conditions to maintain V⁵⁺ and prevent sulfide restabilization

#### Secondary Chemistry Release
- **Does it release any chemicals when forming?** Consumes VO₄³⁻ and OH⁻ from solution; locks up Pb and Zn
- **Byproducts of nucleation:** Minor H⁺ release as OH⁻ is incorporated
- **Byproducts of dissolution:** Releases Pb²⁺, Zn²⁺, VO₄³⁻ — all toxic; dissolution in acid is rapid

#### Growth Characteristics
- **Relative growth rate:** Moderate — faster than vanadinite, slower than cerussite
- **Maximum crystal size:** Individual crystals to ~5 cm (Berg Aukas, Namibia)
- **Typical crystal size in vugs:** 1–20 mm crystals; crusts can cover cm² areas
- **Does growth rate change with temperature?** Slightly faster at warmer temperatures; primarily controlled by vanadate availability
- **Competes with:** Vanadinite (Pb₅(VO₄)₃Cl — grabs Pb and V), wulfenite (PbMoO₄ — grabs Pb if Mo present), cerussite (PbCO₃ — grabs Pb in carbonate-rich fluids), hemimorphite (Zn₄Si₂O₇(OH)₂·H₂O — grabs Zn if Si available)

### Stability
- **Breaks down in heat?** Yes, ~600–700°C → ZnO + lead vanadate melt
- **Breaks down in light?** No known photodecomposition
- **Dissolves in water?** Very low solubility in neutral water (~10⁻⁸ M); Ksp very small
- **Dissolves in acid?** Readily soluble in HCl, HNO₃ — releases Pb²⁺, Zn²⁺, V⁵⁺
- **Oxidizes?** Already fully oxidized (V⁵⁺); stable in oxidizing conditions
- **Dehydrates?** No — only one OH, no water of crystallization
- **Radiation sensitivity:** None known

### Paragenesis
- **Forms AFTER:** Primary sulfides (galena PbS, sphalerite ZnS) have oxidized; vanadium mobilized from wall-rock silicates (micas, clays)
- **Forms BEFORE:** Later carbonation can replace it; vanadinite may overgrow it
- **Commonly associated minerals:** Vanadinite, mottramite, wulfenite, cerussite, anglesite, pyromorphite, mimetite, hemimorphite, limonite/goethite, calcite, barite
- **Zone:** Supergene oxidation zone (above water table, in arid to semi-arid climates)
- **Geological environment:** Oxidized portions of lead-zinc vein deposits in vanadium-bearing country rock; requires arid climate to preserve vanadate minerals (wet climates leach V away)

### Famous Localities
- **Berg Aukas, Namibia** — world's finest descloizite crystals; large spear-point blades to 5 cm, cherry-red to brown
- **Tsumeb, Namibia** — rare but exceptional specimens from the oxidation zone; mottramite dominates there
- **Sierra de Córdoba, Argentina** — type locality (1854)
- **Mapimí, Durango, Mexico** — associated with adamite, hemimorphite, wulfenite
- **Yavasche, Arizona, USA** — small but well-formed crystals
- **Notable specimens:** Berg Aukas produces the largest and most aesthetic crystals known; deep red translucent blades on matrix

### Fluorescence
- **Fluorescent under UV?** Generally no
- **SW (255nm) color:** None
- **MW (310nm) color:** None
- **LW (365nm) color:** None (some impurity-bearing specimens may show very weak response)
- **Phosphorescent?** No
- **Activator:** None standard; V⁵⁺ is typically a quencher
- **Quenched by:** Iron content suppresses any potential fluorescence

### Flavor Text
> Descloizite is what happens when the oxidation zone gets creative with vanadium. Lead and zinc, stripped from their sulfides by groundwater, meet vanadate ions leached from micas in the country rock — and in the right arid climate, they freeze into deep cherry-red blades so heavy they feel wrong in the hand. The zinc-copper series runs red to green: descloizite at one end, mottramite at the other, with every shade of brown in between. At Berg Aukas in Namibia, the crystals grew as spear-point blades up to five centimeters long, translucent, greasy-lustered, stacked in crusts across vug walls. They're vanadate minerals doing their best oxide impression — heavy, dense, and concealing gallium and germanium trace substitutions that nobody expected.

### Simulator Implementation Notes
- **New parameters needed:** trace_V (vanadium as VO₄³⁻) already partially in system; may need explicit V⁵⁺ tracking
- **New events needed:** Vanadium mobilization event from wall-rock weathering (analogous to Mo pulse)
- **Nucleation rule pseudocode:**
```
IF oxidation_zone AND T < 80°C
  AND trace_Pb > 100 AND trace_Zn > 50 AND trace_V > 10
  AND pH 5-8 AND Eh > 0.4V
  AND vug_fill < 1.0
  → nucleate descloizite (max 3 crystals)
```
- **Growth rule pseudocode:**
```
IF σ_descloizite > 1.0 AND T < 80°C
  → grow at rate 4 (moderate)
  → IF trace_Cu > trace_Zn → growth yields mottramite instead
  → IF trace_Cu ≈ trace_Zn → growth yields intermediate "cuprodescloizite"
```
- **Habit selection logic:**
  - T < 30°C AND fast growth → fibrous/botryoidal crust
  - T 30-60°C AND slow growth → tabular prismatic crystals
  - High V concentration → spear-point bladed habit
- **Decomposition products:** ~600°C → ZnO + Pb₂V₂O₇ (lead pyrovanadate glass)

### Variants for Game
- **Variant 1: Cuprodescloizite** — Cu partially replaces Zn, greenish-brown color, found when Cu/Zn ratio approaches 1:1
- **Variant 2: Arsendescloizite** — As substitutes for V, found when As > V in fluid; slightly different properties
- **Variant 3: Crustiform descloizite** — Low-temperature fibrous habit, mammillary surface, forms as layered encrustation rather than distinct crystals

## Paragenetic Sequence Note: Vanadate Oxidation Suite

Descloizite is part of the **lead-vanadium oxidation zone sequence**, sharing formation conditions with:
- **Mottramite** (PbCu(VO₄)(OH)) — Cu-dominant end-member, green
- **Vanadinite** (Pb₅(VO₄)₃Cl) — requires Cl; forms at slightly higher Pb:V ratios
- **Wulfenite** (PbMoO₄) — requires Mo instead of V; often co-occurs when both Mo and V present

The descloizite-mottramite series is a **complete solid solution**: Zn and Cu freely substitute, producing every intermediate composition. Game should track Cu:Zn ratio and color-blend accordingly (red-brown → olive-green).
