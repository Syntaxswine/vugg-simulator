---
species: tyuyamunite
formula: Ca(UO2)2(VO4)2·5-8H2O
description: Orthorhombic Ca-uranyl vanadate. Carnotite's calcium twin — the canary-yellow sandstone stain that takes over wherever groundwater Ca dominates K, which is everywhere except evaporite playas.
---

## blurb

Ca(UO₂)₂(VO₄)₂·5-8H₂O — same chemistry stage as carnotite, opposite cation. Two species, one mechanism, drawn apart by which alkaline-earth/alkali metal happens to dominate the local groundwater. In Colorado Plateau roll-front deposits and Tyuya-Muyun's Fergana Valley sandstones, the two are commonly intergrown, the cation ratio drawing the boundary through the assemblage. Britannica notes they are interconvertible by cation exchange — a tyuyamunite specimen washed with K-rich brine slowly becomes a carnotite, and vice versa. The same yellow, the same powdery sandstone-staining habit, the same diagnostic radioactivity.

## variant: tabular_plates

Rare crystalline tyuyamunite — diamond-shaped plates flattened on {001}, to ~2-3 mm at the type locality (Tyuya-Muyun). Crystalline tyuyamunite is genuinely uncommon outside that one Kyrgyz deposit; almost all tyuyamunite in nature is the powdery or earthy form. Sandstone-hosted uranium deposits don't usually grow good crystals — they stain.

## variant: earthy_crust

Canary-yellow earthy crust — the standard sandstone-staining habit. One percent of tyuyamunite by volume colors entire outcrops the same school-bus yellow that carnotite produces; a hand specimen with both species cannot be told apart by eye. The chemistry assay is the only honest test: Ca-dominant cation = tyuyamunite; K-dominant = carnotite.

## variant: powdery_disseminated

Powdery yellow disseminations — the disseminated sandstone-stain form. Doesn't crystallize so much as it pervades; the yellow is the habit. Most Colorado Plateau tyuyamunite occurs this way, distributed through the host sandstone in concentrations under 1% by volume.

## variant: carnotite_companion

This tyuyamunite is the calcium twin to a carnotite #{} growing in the same pocket. The fluid's K/(K+Ca) ratio decided which one each grain became; specimens preserving both side by side capture a moment when the ratio was crossing 0.5. Geologically, the boundary between them is fluid composition, not chemistry.

## variant: on_weathering_uraninite

Grew on a dissolving uraninite — the canonical paragenetic chain. The reducing-then-oxidizing flip released uranyl into local groundwater that already carried Ca + V from earlier weathering of vanadium-bearing primary minerals (montroseite or roscoelite, neither yet in the simulator). The tyuyamunite precipitated where the three components met.

## variant: roll_front

Roll-front position — concentrated around organic carbon (petrified wood, carbonaceous shale). The simulator doesn't track organic matter as a separate species; we read Fe>5 ppm + low T as a roll-front proxy, since these conditions imply a sandstone host with reducing organic-rich barriers. The Colorado Plateau uranium districts are the classic example: oxidizing groundwater carries U + V + Ca through the sandstone until it hits a carbonaceous reducing zone, where the metals drop out as tyuyamunite (and carnotite, in equal measure).

## variant: acid_dissolution

Acid attack — pH below 5 destabilizes the uranyl-vanadate framework. Below pH 5, vanadate gives way to VO₂⁺ as the dominant V species, the carnotite-group structure can no longer hold, and the crystal goes back to fluid. Ca²⁺ + UO₂²⁺ + VO₄³⁻ released; whatever they find downstream is whatever they become.
