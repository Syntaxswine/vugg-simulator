## blurb

Cu₂AsO₄(OH) — orthorhombic Cu arsenate, the Cu end of the olivenite-adamite series. Olive-green to grayish-green (the Cu chromophore — olive in name and color), Mohs 3-4. Forms in Cu-rich supergene oxidation zones; Cornwall is the type locality, with Tsumeb and Bisbee producing showcase modern specimens. When Zn > Cu in the fluid, adamite (the Zn-end-member of the same arsenate structure) takes priority.

## variant: fibrous

Fibrous — radiating acicular bundles, the high-σ silky habit. The Cornish 'wood-copper' specimens are this form.

## variant: prismatic

Prismatic — the Cornwall display habit. {110} prisms with olive-green tint.

## variant: globular_default

Globular — botryoidal aggregates, the Tsumeb / Bisbee secondary habit.
