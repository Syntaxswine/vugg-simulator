## blurb

γ-FeOOH — the ruby-red platy dimorph of goethite. Same formula, different crystal structure: goethite is a 3D framework (yellow-brown needles), lepidocrocite is layered (ruby-red platy, peels like mica). Kinetically favored when Fe²⁺ oxidizes FAST — e.g. pyrite weathering in situ; slow oxidation produces goethite instead.

## variant: platy_scales

Platy scales — the default habit. 'Lithium quartz' sold in rock shops is quartz with nanoscale lepidocrocite inclusions that scatter pink-mauve through the clear host.

## variant: plumose_rosette

Plumose rosette — radiating platy blades. Best at Cornwall and the Siegerland (Germany).

## variant: fibrous_micaceous

Fibrous micaceous — very rapid growth, coarser particle size, rust-brown color.

## variant: conversion_tail

Given geological time, lepidocrocite converts to goethite (the thermodynamically stable dimorph). This crystal is a freeze-frame of the moment oxidation caught Fe²⁺.
