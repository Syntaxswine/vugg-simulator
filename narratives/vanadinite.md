## blurb

Pb₅(VO₄)₃Cl — hexagonal apatite-group vanadate. Bright red-orange prisms with flat basal terminations, sitting atop goethite-stained matrix. Vanadium-end-member of the pyromorphite–mimetite–vanadinite series, arguably mineralogy's most complete solid-solution triangle.

## variant: endlichite

Endlichite — intermediate vanadinite-mimetite composition with significant As⁵⁺ substituting for V⁵⁺. The color shifts toward yellow as As dominates. The compositional series is continuous.

## variant: red_signature

The signature red-orange. This is the chromophore pegged to V⁵⁺ in the crystal structure — no other common mineral produces this particular red. The Moroccan Mibladen and Touissit deposits have produced the world's finest specimens, growing on goethite crust in near-surface oxidation pockets.

## variant: desert_tail

Classic desert mineral. V comes from oxidation of V-bearing red-bed sediments (roll-front uranium deposits, ironstones) — an arid-climate signature. The rock-shop cliché 'vanadinite on goethite' is geologically accurate.

## variant: vanadate_companions

Vanadate companions — {companions} share this vug. Descloizite (PbZnVO₄(OH)) and mottramite (PbCu(VO₄)(OH)) are both Pb vanadates that draw their V from the same red-bed source as vanadinite, but partner with Zn and Cu respectively instead of binding to Cl⁻. Tsumeb is the type for the full vanadinite + descloizite + mottramite trio — three minerals recording three different cation availabilities in the same supergene cascade.
