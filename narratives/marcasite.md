---
species: marcasite
formula: FeS2
description: Iron disulfide. The orthorhombic FeS₂ dimorph (pyrite is cubic); the unstable, acid-loving polymorph that converts to pyrite over geologic time.
---

## variant: cockscomb

The crystal developed the classic cockscomb habit — aggregated tabular plates on {010}, edges ridged like a rooster's comb. This shape is the diagnostic fingerprint: pyrite never crests like this.

## variant: spearhead

Spearhead twins — paired tabular crystals tapered to pyramidal tips. The {101} twin law produces a swallowtail shape unique to marcasite.

## variant: radiating_blade

Radiating blades sprayed outward from a common center — low-temperature, high-supersaturation growth in acid fluids, the same style that gives sedimentary marcasite nodules their stellate fracture patterns.

## variant: tabular_plates

Flat tabular {010} plates — the slow-growth marcasite form, pale brass already starting to iridesce as surface sulfur oxidizes.

## variant: twinned

Shows the {twin_law} swallowtail twin, diagnostic of marcasite and absent from its cubic cousin pyrite.

## variant: dissolved_inversion

Metastable inversion or oxidative breakdown destroyed the crystal — marcasite is the unstable FeS₂ dimorph. Over geologic time it converts to pyrite; on museum shelves it rots to sulfuric acid and iron sulfate.

## variant: kept_orthorhombic

The pH/T regime kept it in the orthorhombic field; given geologic time or a temperature excursion above 240°C, this crystal would invert to pyrite.
