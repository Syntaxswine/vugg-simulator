## blurb

Pb(Cu,Zn)VO₄(OH) — orthorhombic Pb-Cu vanadate, the Cu end of the descloizite-mottramite series. Olive-green to yellowish-green from the Cu chromophore, distinguishing it at a glance from cherry-red descloizite. Type locality: Mottram St. Andrew, Cheshire (England), 1876. Tsumeb produces the museum-grade specimens. When Zn ≥ Cu in the fluid, descloizite takes priority instead.

## variant: botryoidal

Botryoidal — concentric olive-green layers, the Mottram St. Andrew type habit.

## variant: prismatic

Prismatic — Tsumeb's olive-green crystals; the world's finest mottramite specimens.

## variant: tabular_default

Tabular — late-stage habit, olive plates on the wall.
