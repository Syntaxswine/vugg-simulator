species: sapphire
formula: Al2O3
description: Multi-color non-red corundum. Fe/Ti/V chromophores; the redox+IVCT record of every fluid that ever sat in the pocket.

## blurb

Al₂O₃ with Fe/Ti/V trace — the non-red corundum varieties. Color comes from the specific chromophore or charge-transfer mechanism: blue from Fe²⁺-Ti⁴⁺ intervalence charge transfer (IVCT), yellow from Fe³⁺ alone, violet from V³⁺, pink from sub-ruby Cr, padparadscha from Cr + trace Fe. Shares the SiO₂-undersaturated upper gate with corundum + ruby.

## variant: cornflower

Cornflower blue — the Kashmir type. Mined out 1880s-1930s; remaining specimens are heirloom auction pieces. The 'velvet' appearance comes from microscale silk inclusions scattering transmitted light.

## variant: royal_blue

Royal blue — deeper Fe than Kashmir cornflower, more saturated. Burma and Ceylon classic.

## variant: padparadscha

Padparadscha — the pink-orange corundum named for the Sinhalese word for lotus blossom. Exquisite and rare; Sri Lanka is the historical source, Madagascar modern. The Cr + trace Fe chemistry is on a knife edge between pink sapphire and padparadscha.

## variant: yellow

Yellow sapphire — Fe³⁺ in the Al site, no Ti partner. Sri Lanka + Montana Yogo are the classic sources; Yogo yellow + blue co-occur in the same basaltic host.

## variant: violet

Violet sapphire — V³⁺ chromophore, Tanzania signature. Umba Valley is the color-change locality where Cr + V combine in a crystal that shifts from blue (daylight) to purple (incandescent).

## variant: pink

Pink sapphire — Cr just below the 2 ppm ruby gate. Industry boundary case; some trade houses call it ruby, others pink sapphire. The gem language is contentious.

## variant: green

Green sapphire — Fe alone, no Ti partner. Australia + Montana alluvial deposits are the sources. Less commercially prized than blue or yellow.

## variant: asterated

6-rayed star sapphire — rutile needles aligned along basal plane. Sri Lanka star blue and Burma black star are the famous cabochon gems.

## variant: barrel

Steep dipyramidal 'barrel' — basalt-hosted xenocryst signature. Montana Yogo, Thailand/Cambodia, and Australia basalt-hosted sapphire mostly crystallize in this habit.

## variant: tabular

Flat hexagonal plate — Mogok marble-hosted, the dominant habit in metamorphic contact sapphire.
