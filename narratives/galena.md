## blurb

PbS — the densest common sulfide (SG 7.6), perfect cubic cleavage, bright lead-gray metallic luster. Pick up a piece and it's surprisingly heavy; tap it and it cleaves into perfect little cubes.

## variant: spinel_twin

Twinned on the {twin_law} — spinel-law twins create striking interpenetrating cubes in galena, rare but diagnostic.

## variant: argentiferous

The fluid carried silver — argentiferous galena, the historic source of most of the world's silver (Potosí, Leadville, Broken Hill).

## variant: oxidative_breakdown

Oxidation attacked the galena — Pb²⁺ went into solution and can reprecipitate as cerussite (PbCO₃), anglesite (PbSO₄), or — if Mo is present — wulfenite (PbMoO₄).
