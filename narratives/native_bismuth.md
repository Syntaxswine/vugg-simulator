## blurb

Bi — elemental bismuth. Silver-white on fresh fracture, iridescent rainbow tarnish within hours. Melts at an unusually low 271.5°C — the lowest melting point of any native metal. Only forms when sulfur runs out before bismuth does (otherwise it makes bismuthinite). The square hoppered rainbow Bi crystals sold in rock shops are LAB-GROWN — natural native bismuth is typically arborescent or massive.

## variant: arborescent_dendritic

Arborescent dendritic — tree-like branches filling a fracture. Cobalt (Germany), the Kingsgate mine (Australia), and Schneeberg (Saxony) produced the best historically.

## variant: rhombohedral_crystal

Rhombohedral crystal — RARE. Bismuth crystallizes in trigonal symmetry with {0001} basal pinacoid; well-formed natural crystals are among the rarest native-element specimens collectors seek.

## variant: massive_default

Massive granular — silver-white metallic blob, iridescent within days of exposure to air.
