---
species: brochantite
formula: Cu4(SO4)(OH)6
description: Wet-supergene Cu sulfate. The higher-pH end of the brochantite-antlerite fork; the Statue of Liberty's actual patina mineral.
---

## blurb

Cu₄(SO₄)(OH)₆ — the wet-supergene Cu sulfate. Emerald-green prismatic crystals, distinguishable from malachite by distinctly darker green and prismatic (vs malachite's botryoidal) habit. The higher-pH end (pH 4-7) of the brochantite ↔ antlerite Cu-sulfate fork. Atacama Desert (Chile) supergene Cu deposits — Chuquicamata, Mantos Blancos, Mansa Mina, El Tesoro — have brochantite as a major component; arid evaporative concentration of supergene Cu sulfate produces near-pure brochantite zones.

## variant: drusy_crust

Drusy emerald-green crust on Cu-bearing wall — the rapid-supergene-precipitation habit. Atacama and Bisbee tailings dumps stain green with this in days to weeks of post-mining oxidation.

## variant: acicular_tuft

Acicular needle-tufts radiating from substrate — the diagnostic habit when brochantite tufts coat malachite or chalcocite.

## variant: short_prismatic

Stubby emerald-green prisms — the standard Atacama / Bisbee display specimen habit. Hand-lens reveals monoclinic crystal forms.

## variant: botryoidal_default

Botryoidal globular aggregates — rarer than the prismatic habit, can be confused with malachite at hand-sample scale (the green color and globular form overlap; XRD or acid-resistance test distinguishes).

## variant: cl_rich

Cl-rich fluid context: in real-life Atacama and Bisbee, brochantite competes with atacamite (Cu₂Cl(OH)₃) for the Cu²⁺ pool — atacamite wins when Cl is dominant over SO₄. Atacamite is queued for a future halide-expansion commit.

## variant: dissolved_pH_fork

Dissolved by {cause}. The brochantite ↔ antlerite fork is the single most-cited Cu-sulfate paragenesis in supergene literature (Pollard et al. 1992); both fork ends can interconvert as pH cycles seasonally.

## variant: patina_tail

Patina-mineralogy connection: bronze sculptures in oceanic / saline air develop brochantite patinas (vs malachite in CO₂-rich freshwater air). The Statue of Liberty's iconic green patina is largely brochantite, not malachite — chloride-rich New York harbor air drives the SO₄/CO₃ partition toward sulfate.
