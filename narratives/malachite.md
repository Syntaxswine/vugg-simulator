## variant: on_chalcopyrite

It nucleated directly on chalcopyrite — the classic oxidation paragenesis. As oxygenated water attacked the copper sulfide, Cu²⁺ was liberated and combined with carbonate to form malachite. This is the green stain that led ancient prospectors to copper deposits.

## variant: banded

The crystal developed the famous banded texture — concentric layers of alternating light and dark green, each band recording a pulse of growth. This is the texture prized in decorative stonework since the Bronze Age, from Russian palaces to Egyptian amulets.

## variant: botryoidal

Botryoidal habit — smooth, rounded green masses that gleam like polished jade. Cross-sections would reveal concentric banding.

## variant: fibrous_acicular

Rapid growth produced fibrous, acicular malachite — sprays of needle-like green crystals radiating from nucleation points. Delicate and sparkling, a different beauty from the massive variety.

## variant: acid_dissolution

Acid attack dissolved some malachite — it fizzes in acid like calcite, releasing Cu²⁺ and CO₂. The green stain on the fingers of anyone who handles it with acidic sweat.
