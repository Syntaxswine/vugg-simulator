## blurb

Brassy yellow with a greenish tint — distinguishable from pyrite by its deeper color and softer hardness (3.5 vs 6). The disphenoidal crystals often look tetrahedral, a common misidentification.

## variant: twinned

Shows {twin_law} twinning — repeated twins create spinel-like star shapes.

## variant: dissolved

Oxidation began converting the chalcopyrite — at the surface, this weathering produces malachite (green) and azurite (blue), the colorful signal that led ancient prospectors to copper deposits.
