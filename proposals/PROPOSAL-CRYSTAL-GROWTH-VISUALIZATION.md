# PROPOSAL: Crystal Growth History Visualization (The Specimen Card)

**Date:** 2026-04-23
**Status:** Research proposal — vision from Professor
**Priority:** Visual — potentially THE signature visual feature

---

## The Idea (Professor's vision)

Each crystal's growth history is rendered INSIDE the crystal's actual shape outline. The bands follow the crystal habit — cubic minerals get nested zones, prismatic minerals get elongated bands, botryoidal minerals get concentric shells. The shape IS the chart. The chart IS the specimen.

## Where It Lives

- **Inventory view** — every collected crystal shows its growth history as a specimen card. Tap to expand and see broth chemistry at each band.
- **Library view** — same visualization, grouped by species, showing how different specimens of the same mineral can tell completely different growth stories.
- **During growth** — live rendering as the crystal grows, bands appearing in real-time as the broth chemistry shifts.

## Shape-Band Mapping

The band geometry follows the crystal habit:

- **Cubic** (fluorite, halite, galena, pyrite): nested concentric cubes — the classic "phantom" look
- **Hexagonal prismatic** (quartz, tourmaline, beryl): elongated zones running the length — like real amethyst or watermelon tourmaline cross-sections
- **Rhombohedral** (calcite, rhodochrosite, dolomite): diamond-shaped nested zones — the "button" look
- **Botryoidal** (malachite, chrysocolla): concentric shells — like sliced stalactitic specimens
- **Tabular** (barite, wulfenite): flat layered zones — like pages in a book
- **Acicular** (rutile, tourmaline needles): thin bands along the needle axis

## The Moment That Sold It

Professor: "I realized it could go inside crystal shapes to form a natural image."

Not a bar chart next to a crystal. The growth history IS the crystal. Two quartz crystals from the same vug could have completely different stories written inside them, visible at a glance. The crystal tells you its own story.
