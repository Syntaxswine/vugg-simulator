## 2. The science (verified 2026-07-10; two passes reconciled)

**The governing variable is one number: cumulative misorientation.** Growth splitting is a
recognized, continuous morphological series whose single ordering parameter is the **total
misorientation angle** spanned by a crystal's subindividuals — the sum of many small
non-crystallographic branching events (Shtukenberg, Punin, Gunn & Kahr, *Spherulites*, **Chem. Rev.
2012, 112(3), 1805–1838**, DOI 10.1021/cr200297f — verified exact by both passes). The rungs:

| rung | form | distinguishing criterion |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | single polyhedral crystal | zero cumulative misorientation; layer/spiral growth, low driving force |
| **1** | **bent / curved crystal** — saddle dolomite, curved barite, bent/"ram's-horn" gypsum | **small, CONTINUOUS** misorientation (fractions of a ° → a few °) as lattice curvature; **sweeping extinction**; bending, not yet branching |
| 2 | split crystal, few subindividuals — fishtail/swallowtail gypsum, split calcite | **discrete** branching into a few subgrains; a parent with splayed terminations |
| 3 | sheaf-of-wheat / bowtie (German *Garbe*) | cumulative divergence ~90–180°; both ends fan, a central "waist" survives |
| 4 | spherulite | ~180° (hemisphere) → ~360° (sphere); radial fibres, **Maltese-cross extinction**; single-crystal coherence lost |

The spherulite "is closely related to the splitting of crystals" — the sheaf is the sphere
half-built. (Encode the cross-cutting distinction too: **Category 1** = radial from the nucleus;
**Category 2** = grows *through* fibre→sheaf→bowtie→sphere. The ladder above is Cat-2 ontogeny frozen
at stages. Morse & Donnay 1936; Keith & Padden 1963.)

**THREE mechanism CLASSES that look alike and must NOT be conflated** (the content pass's central
warning):
- **A — impurity/additive-induced split growth** (Shtukenberg–Punin *autodeformation*): additive
  incorporation → coherency strain → the lattice bends elastically, then *releases* the stored strain
  by splitting into misoriented subindividuals. **Impurity is the driver; can fire at LOW σ.**
- **B — spherulitic crystallisation from high supersaturation / gel / viscous media**: fast
  radial-fibrous growth, **impurity NOT required**, kinetic/diffusion-limited (Mullins–Sekerka
  interface instability + Keith–Padden cellulation + Gránásy growth-front nucleation).
- **C — concentric botryoidal / colloform accretion**: rhythmic layering of **many** nuclei — **NOT
  single-crystal splitting** (each botryoid is usually radial-fibrous inside = a B micro-texture in a
  C macro-form). Malachite, kidney-ore hematite, banded rhodochrosite. **O5-splitting is A + B, never
  C** (C is aggregation, a different rung the sim already paints as botryoidal habits).

**The driver has a NUMBER — but the two routes pull OPPOSITE ways on σ (the key design fact).**
- **B-route:** far-from-equilibrium onset. **Beck & Andreassen 2010 measured spherulitic CaCO₃
  setting in above a saturation index SI ≈ 2–3** (S ≈ few-hundred to a thousand): below → faceted
  single crystals, above → spherulites through the split/sheaf intermediates. Sunagawa's morphodrom
  (polyhedral → hopper → dendritic → split/spherulitic with rising driving force) is the same story.
  That SI≈2–3 is a **carbonate** number — and carbonates are the fleet's core, so it's the calibration
  anchor (§4).
- **A-route:** the *opposite* σ-sign. Punin & Artamonova 2001 show gypsum autodeformation bending
  **strengthens at LOWER supersaturation** — impurity, not σ, climbs it. So a single "more σ = more
  splitting" law would be WRONG: the low rungs (curved/saddle, A) and the high rungs (spherulite, B)
  are driven by **different levers**. This is not a complication to hide; it is the honest two-route
  structure the mechanism adopts (§4), and it echoes the sim's two-route sceptre.

**Splitting ability is crystal-structure-specific** — carbonates, zeolites, some phosphates
(wavellite) split readily; framework silicates (quartz, feldspar) barely at all — so this is a
**per-mineral gate**, not a fleet-wide law.

**HONESTY CLAUSE (the σ*(φ) lesson, reused).** The *microphysics of branching* — WHY a growing
crystal splits its lattice by small angles — **is explicitly an open question** (the Chem. Rev.
review says the mechanism "is often not addressed"). The candidate models are real but competing —
Keith–Padden (impurity segregation + cellulation), Gránásy growth-front nucleation, Punin–Shtukenberg
autodeformation. The autodeformation frame carries the unifying claim we build on: **bending
(continuous) and splitting (discrete) are the SAME growth-actuated stress-relaxation phenomenon** —
which is exactly why saddle dolomite and a spherulite sit on ONE continuum. O5-splitting renders the
**phenomenon and its one-index ladder**, not a crowned microphysics — the discipline that let σ*(φ)
ship on a threshold-shape without choosing Cabrera–Vermilyea vs kink-blocking vs cooperative
adsorption.

**Saddle dolomite — the low rung, and the inference stated AS an inference.** Saddle/baroque dolomite
(coarse dolospar, curved boundaries + sweeping extinction, FI Th commonly 90–160 °C) falls
**naturally at Rung 1** in the autodeformation framework; Grigor'ev's *Ontogeny of Minerals* roots
the radial series in bending/splitting with a "chemical splitting" by foreign ions (Mg alongside Ca —
germane to Ca-excess dolomite). **CAVEAT, plainly: no explicit published sentence "saddle dolomite =
grade-1 splitting" was found** (the Punin/Shtukenberg texts are paywalled) — treat it as *inference
consistent with the framework, not a verified quote.* The dolomite literature weights it differently
but **not incompatibly**: Radke & Mathis 1980 (rapid disequilibrium growth, Ca-excess, elevated T);
Barber, Reeder & Smith 1985 TEM (curvature from coherent {104} calcite micro-lamellae whose lattice
mismatch distorts the host — a *specific realisation* of "mismatch → strain → curvature"); Searl 1989
(edge-nucleated Mg-rich wedges). The sim adopts saddle dolomite as the phenomenon's low rung while
keeping its delivery-limited driver (C1 depletion) and — critically — its distinctness from the sim's
existing **shear-bent** saddle (§3.4): three causes for one shape, kept honest by census.

## 4. The mechanism (design)

**State (per crystal):** `_split = { index, rung, route, driver }` — `index` a 0..1 cumulative
misorientation scalar; `rung` ∈ {none, curved, split, sheaf, spherulite}; `route` ∈ {A, B, both} for
narration + the two-mechanism invariant; `driver` the σ / impurity trace. Serialised additively
(pre-bump saves read `_split` undefined = none, byte-safe).

**Two routes, one index (the A/B honesty from §2 — the design's spine):**
```
splitAbility(mineral) ∈ [0,1]          // structure-specific gate; 0 for quartz/feldspar
impurity_factor        ∈ [0,1]         // = max(film φ, scenario trace, mineral hint); _film wired FIRST (§9a boss verdict)
sigma_local                             // the crystal's own cell σ (C1)

rateA = SPLIT_K_A · impurity_factor · splitAbility            // autodeformation: impurity-driven, σ-agnostic (even LOW σ)
rateB = SPLIT_K_B · max(0, sigma_local − sigma_spherulite*) · splitAbility   // high-σ spherulitic
_split.index = clamp01( Σ (rateA + rateB) · dgrowth )
_split.rung  = band(index)   // e.g. 0.08 curved / 0.25 split / 0.55 sheaf / 0.85 spherulite
_split.route = which of rateA/rateB dominated the accumulation
```
`sigma_spherulite*` is the sim-σ image of SI ≈ 2–3 (Beck & Andreassen; calibrate on the sim's own σ
scale by the 4a.7 recipe). **Byte-identity for the untouched fleet falls out of the arithmetic:** a
crystal with `splitAbility = 0`, OR with `impurity_factor = 0` AND `sigma_local ≤ sigma_spherulite*`,
accrues rate 0 → index frozen at 0 → identical to today, no `if` guard. The split set is exactly
{splitting-able minerals that are either dusted/impure (A) or far-from-equilibrium (B)} — census-bounded
(S-a), the O4b/O5b certificate pattern.

**Earning the ladder:** `rung` drives the existing render params —
- **curved** → `_makeSaddleRhomb` curvature = f(index) (retire the painted mild constant *for the
  split-driven set*; the shear-saddle set keeps its own curvature source, uncolliding);
- **sheaf** → the hemimorphite-fan splay angle = f(index);
- **spherulite** → radial completeness / cluster-satellite fan fraction = f(index).
and routes the habit choice, replacing keyword scoring.

**Module grain (the O5-masking answer, reused):** the index accrual lives in the growth path (js/85,
beside the O3b `_buried` throttle and the O5b σ*(φ) gate — the growth-modifiers sit together); the
`rung` tag + habit route live where habit is chosen (js/07 / js/27); the render read lives in js/99i
beside `_makeSaddleRhomb` and the fan.
