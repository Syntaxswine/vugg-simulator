## 0. THE HEADLINE (read this first)

**None of §8's four named tenants is driven by an external tectonic SHEAR FIELD
that a crystal integrates as it grows.** §8 conflated three physically distinct
categories. The honest design is NOT one shear field; it is (at least) two
different mechanics, plus a correction to the gwindel home.

| §8 tenant | §8 assumed | Science says | Category |
|---|---|---|---|
| Gwindel twist | tectonic shear | screw-dislocation **Eshelby twist** in **spiral growth**, about an *a*-axis | GROWTH-DEFECT (not shear) |
| Saddle dolomite | shear curvature | grown-in lattice strain: Ca-excess + **surface roughening >~50–60 °C** + calcitic ribbons | GROWTH-DEFECT, **T-driven** (not shear) |
| Bent crystals | one bending field | gypsum=syn-growth; cylindrite=intrinsic structure; **quartz/stibnite/mica = post-growth** | SPLIT (mostly OVERPRINT) |
| Deformation lamellae / twins | growth-zone tag | calcite e-twins, quartz lamellae = imposed on a **finished** lattice | POST-GROWTH OVERPRINT |

The single genuine "integrate stress during growth" case — **syntectonic
porphyroblasts (snowball garnet)** — was not on the §8 list, and its mechanism is
contested.

**Implication:** building §8 literally would re-pin gwindel from an honest

## 1. Gwindel twist — GROWTH-DEFECT, not shear

**Mechanism (consensus, single rigorous source):** the twist is a distribution of
straight screw dislocations (b = 1/3⟨2̄110⟩, density ~10¹⁰ m⁻²) producing an
**Eshelby twist**; the microstructure implies the crystal formed by the **spiral
growth mechanism** (growth under small driving forces). This is intrinsically a
*growth* model.

- **Cordier, P. & Heidelbach, F. (2013).** "Origin of twist in 'gwindel' quartz
  crystals from the Alps: a transmission electron microscopy study." *Eur. J.
  Mineral.* 25(2):145–153. **DOI 10.1127/0935-1221/2013/0025-2271.** VERIFIED
  (abstract read verbatim from Schweizerbart). *The keystone.*
- General Eshelby-twist physics (not gwindel-specific, both verified): Eshelby
  (1953) *J. Appl. Phys.* 24:176; Bierman et al. (2008) *Science* 320:1060,
  DOI 10.1126/science.1157131 (Eshelby twist demonstrated in growing nanowires).

**Geometry:** twist is rotation **about the flattening *a*-axis**, NOT the c-axis;
~"a few degrees per centimetre" (verbatim), classic ~1–5°/cm (Frondel 1962;
Rykart 1989). **Handedness = quartz enantiomorphism** (right-handed → clockwise).
Dauphiné twinning *can* coexist (same hand) but is NOT the cause; Brazil twinning
cannot make a gwindel (opposite hands → conflicting twist). Twist is a continuous
single-crystal lattice curvature, not a stack of rotated subcrystals (the "deck of
cards" look is a secondary surface/overgrowth effect).

**Honest caveat:** the 2013 paper proposes a growth mechanism and is *silent* on
tectonic stress — it does not contain a sentence explicitly "ruling out" shear.
n=2 crystals. So this is consensus-by-default (one rigorous, uncontested study),
not consensus-by-replication. But "growth, not shear" is the sound reading, and a
shear-field home would assert a cause the literature does not.

**Distinct neighbours (do not conflate):**
- **Tessin habit** = a *face* habit (steep rhombs, prism suppressed); about
  morphology, not twist. (vugg already ships this on grimsel.)
- **Fadenquartz** = crack-seal fissure healing — *this* one is genuinely tectonic
  (and is the real home of "shear/strain" storytelling for quartz), but it is a
  white inclusion thread, mechanistically unrelated to gwindel twist. Recent:
  "Rapid crack-seal growth of Faden quartz" (2025) *J. Struct. Geol.* (PII
  S0191814125000070; full cite unverified).

**Could not verify:** a "Weiss" gwindel article (real item is extraLapis Nr. 3
"Bergkristall", Weise ed. 1992); a Stalder *twist* paper (the real Stalder ref is
the 1998 *Mineralienlexikon der Schweiz*); the ">13°/cm" numeral; Reusch (1882)
eddy hypothesis (real but superseded, primary unseen).

## 2. Saddle (baroque) dolomite — GROWTH-DEFECT, T-driven, not shear

**Mechanism (consensus the curvature is a GROWTH feature; the specific driver is a
multi-hypothesis (b)-tier):**
- **Radke & Mathis (1980)** *J. Sed. Petrol.* 50(4):1149–1168 — founding paper;
  Ca-excess compositional gradients along growth laminae → trigonal lattice
  distortion = saddle morphology. VERIFIED (GeoScienceWorld).
- **Gregg & Sibley (1984)** *J. Sed. Petrol.* 54(3):908–931, DOI
  10.1306/212F8535-2B24-11D7-8648000102C1865D — **critical roughening temperature
  (CRT ~50 °C)**: above it, nonplanar (curved) growth is favoured; below, planar.
  VERIFIED. *This is the most "lawlike" control: degree of curvature rises with T
  above the CRT.*
- **Barber, Reeder & Smith (1985)** *Contrib. Mineral. Petrol.* 91(1):82–92, DOI
  10.1007/BF00429430 — TEM: branching *calcitic ribbon* defects (3.8–6.7% larger
  spacing) impose internal lattice distortion that manifests macroscopically as
  curved faces. VERIFIED. **The decisive growth-not-deformation evidence:**
  curvature is built into the internal microstructure during aqueous growth,
  incompatible with a post-growth external-stress origin.
- **Searl (1989)** *Mineral. Mag.* 53(373):547–555, DOI
  10.1180/minmag.1989.053.373.05 — Mg-enriched wedges, rapid transport-controlled
  growth. VERIFIED. (Disagrees on the *sign* of the compositional anomaly, agrees
  on the *category*: growth/transport.)

**Conditions:** elevated T (classic >60 °C, CRT ~50 °C, fluid-inclusion clusters
~90–160 °C); warm saline brines; **MVT base-metal deposits** (classic gangue),
burial & hydrothermal/fault-controlled. Note Spötl & Pitman (1998, IAS SP26)
reappraisal: it is *elevated*-T but not a precise stand-alone geothermometer; low-T
occurrences exist — treat the threshold as a guide.

**Degree of curvature:** qualitative consensus only — rises with T above CRT, with
Ca-excess/defect density, and with growth rate. **No published quantitative
curvature law.** Don't claim one.

**Could not verify:** a specific Machel quote on curvature (he's a major dolomite
author but I did not confirm exact wording); Sibley & Gregg 1987 texture-
classification details; Spötl & Pitman exact pages.

**For vugg:** saddle dolomite needs the **temperature** the sim already tracks
(roughening above ~50–60 °C) + a **Ca-excess / growth-rate** read — NOT a shear
field. It is a sibling of the shipped calcite-morphology arc, not a deformation
mechanic. **And vugg already has MVT scenarios (mvt, elmwood lineage)** — there is
a ready content home.

## 3. Bent / curved crystals — SPLITS three ways

| Mineral | Category | Mechanism | Citation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Gypsum / selenite "ram's horn" | **A syn-growth** (B-flavoured trigger) | impurity (Mg²⁺/Cd²⁺) misfit stress released by cracking + mechanical twinning *at the growth front*, bends as it lengthens | Rinaudo, Franchini-Angela & Boistelle (1989) *Mineral. Mag.* 53(372):479–482, DOI 10.1180/minmag.1989.053.372.08 — VERIFIED |
| Cylindrite (Pb-Sn-Sb-Fe) | **B intrinsic** | incommensurate **misfit-layer** composite; H/T sheet mismatch → progressive rotation/curving rolls the crystal | Wang & Buseck (1992) *Am. Mineral.* 77:758–764 — cite VERIFIED (abstract via aggregation) |
| Bent quartz (plain) | **C post-growth** | bend-gliding / dislocation creep warps a finished lattice (undulose extinction = diagnostic) | structural-geology consensus; syn-growth class reviewed in Shtukenberg 2014 |
| Stibnite (Sb₂S₃) blades | **C post-growth** | translation gliding on (010)[001] → slip/kink bands; the same glide makes blades bendable | McQueen, Kelly & Clark (1980) *Tectonophysics* (vol/pages **unconfirmed**, mechanism confident) |
| Mica "books" (biotite/musc.) | **C post-growth** | basal-plane slip localizes into kink bands under compression | consensus; exact canonical paper **not locked** |
| Curved millerite / capillary fibres | **A syn-growth** (inference) | anisotropic acicular syn-growth; sagenite is enclosed, not deformed | inference + Shtukenberg 2014 |

**Master review (verified):** Shtukenberg, Punin, Gujral & Kahr (2014) "Growth
Actuated Bending and Twisting of Single Crystals," *Angew. Chem. Int. Ed.*
53(3):672–699, DOI 10.1002/anie.201301223. The authoritative anchor for the
syn-growth (A/B) bending family.

**The deliverable:** integrable-during-growth = gypsum, cylindrite, capillary
fibres. **Overprint (grew straight, bent later, NOT integrable) = bent quartz,
stibnite, mica.** The latter need a *separate deformation pass on a finished
crystal*, not a growth-field integration.

## 4. Mechanical twins & deformation lamellae — POST-GROWTH OVERPRINTS (confirmed)

**Confirmed, textbook consensus:** calcite e-twins {01-12} and quartz deformation
lamellae / undulose extinction / subgrains / dynamic recrystallization are
**post-growth crystal-plastic overprints** imposed on an already-grown lattice —
which is precisely why they work as paleostress/paleostrain gauges. A growth-
integration field **cannot** model them.

- Calcite: Turner (1953) *Am. J. Sci.* 251:276 (dynamic analysis); Groshong (1972)
  *GSA Bull.* 83:2025 (strain gauge); **Burkhard (1993)** *JSG* 15:351 (twin
  morphology↔stress/T); **Ferrill et al. (2004)** *JSG* 26:1521 (Type I–IV
  morphology geothermometer: I <~200 °C … IV >~250 °C). All VERIFIED.
- Quartz: Hirth & Tullis (1992) *JSG* 14:145 (creep regimes 1–3); **Stipp et al.
  (2002)** *JSG* 24:1861 (BLG ~280–400 / SGR ~400–500 / GBM >~500 °C) + companion
  Geol. Soc. SP 200:171. All VERIFIED. (Absolute T-brackets are calibrations,
  strain-rate/water dependent — semi-quantitative.)
- Concept split: Passchier & Trouw, *Microtectonics* (2005, DOI 10.1007/3-540-
  29359-0); Vernon, *Practical Guide to Rock Microstructure* (2004/2018). VERIFIED.
  Growth twin (during formation) vs deformation/mechanical twin (stress, after) is
  explicit and standard.

**The genuine syn-growth-under-stress case — syntectonic porphyroblasts:** rotated
/ "snowball" garnet with spiral inclusion trails records deformation *as it grows*
by trapping the evolving matrix fabric (Si vs Se; Zwart 1962). **This is the only
phenomenon that fits "integrate a stress field during growth."** But it is
contested: classical rotation (Rosenfeld 1970, GSA SP129) vs Bell & Johnson
non-rotation/crenulation (Bell 1985 *JMG* 3:109; Bell & Johnson 1989 *JMG* 7:279).
Present both; pick no winner. Render = inclusion-trail spirals (hard).

## 5. THE REFRAMED DESIGN (what to build, science-first)

§8's "one shear field" is retired. The honest decomposition is **three separate
mechanics**, ranked by value × honesty × cost:

### 5.1 Saddle dolomite — ✅ SHIPPED (render-only, SIM-neutral, 2026-06-20)
**DONE.** The engine already tagged the `saddle_rhomb` habit (and correctly —
`tools/saddle-dolomite-observe.mjs` proved it fires ONLY in warm hydrothermal
`reactive_wall` @ 95–133 °C; ambient sabkha/dripstone/supergene @ 25 °C stay
`massive`, so the roughening-T science gate is already in `grow_dolomite` — no
classifier correction needed). What was missing was the RENDER: `saddle_rhomb`
missed the `'rhombohedral'` substring in `_habitGeomToken` and fell to the
default hex prism (a token wart). Added `js/99i _makeSaddleRhomb(curvature)` — a
curved-face rhombohedron (each of the 6 {104} faces grid-subdivided and bowed
convexly outward along its normal, pinned to zero at the edges so the solid
stays watertight; 576-vertex signature) + a mesh-sync hook gated on the habit
string, bow amplitude keyed gently to growth T (Gregg & Sibley CRT). RENDER-ONLY
→ baseline byte-identical (still `seed42_v207`), SIM-NEUTRAL (the render-wart /
sonifier precedent). Verified in the preview Three renderer (curved gold saddle
vs flat blue rhomb, no console errors). Test: `tests-js/saddle-dolomite.test.ts`
pins the premise (warm→saddle, ambient→planar). Render groundwork now exists for
the §5.3 overprint arc's bend/curve transforms.

### 5.1-orig Saddle dolomite — RECOMMENDED FIRST (cleanest win)
- **Driver it actually has:** temperature (roughening above ~50–60 °C) + Ca-excess
  / growth-rate. The sim already tracks T and fluid Ca/Mg. **No new field needed.**
- **Mechanic shape:** a growth-defect *morphology classifier*, sibling to the
  shipped calcite-morphology arc (js/45 + a curved-face render). Reuse
  `_makeGwindelGeom`'s SEG loop, but perturb *face normals* (curvature) instead of
  twisting the long axis.
- **Content home:** existing MVT scenarios (mvt / elmwood lineage) — saddle
  dolomite is the classic MVT gangue. Possibly a dedicated hydrothermal-dolomite
  beat.
- **Honesty:** fully defensible — it IS a T-driven growth-defect, modelled on its
  real driver. No confabulation.

### 5.2 Gwindel — CORRECT THE HOME (do NOT re-pin on shear)
- Keep gwindel as a **habit-variant** (the v207 framing is honest). If refined,
  key it on **spiral-growth conditions** (small driving force / low σ, long growth
  duration → accumulated Eshelby twist), NOT a shear field. Twist ∝ growth extent
  is the *correct* abstraction per Cordier & Heidelbach.
- This is a small refinement at most — possibly leave v207 as-is. The science says
  the shear-field re-pin (§8.5 step 2) is WRONG; that step is struck.

### 5.3 Post-growth deformation OVERPRINT — the genuine "real deformation" arc
- This is the architecturally-new mechanic the boss actually asked for, and it is
  **NOT** a grow-integrate field. It is a **post-processing pass** that takes a
  *finished* crystal and applies a deformation from a scenario **deformation
  event** (a D2/D3 tectonic beat on the timeline, after growth):
  - **bend** (bent quartz, stibnite blades, mica kinks) — per-segment lateral
    offset on the finished mesh.
  - **mechanical twin lamellae** (calcite e-twins) — a zone/overlay tag + render
    lamellae; density keyed to event stress (Ferrill Type I–IV ↔ T).
  - **deformation lamellae / undulose** (quartz) — strain-band overlay tag.
- This honestly models the structural-geology storytelling axis §8 wanted — but as
  an *overprint on a grown crystal*, which is what the science demands. Real
  homes: alpine/MVT scenarios with a late tectonic event; fadenquartz (crack-seal)
  is the genuinely-tectonic quartz tenant if quartz is wanted here.

**✅ STEP 3 TENANT 1 SHIPPED — BENT QUARTZ @ TORMIQ (SIM 208, 2026-06-20).** The
overprint architecture is built and proven, render-and-all:
- A scenario event carries a `deformation` directive `{style,magnitude,minerals}`

- Render: `js/99i _makeBentPrism` arcs the long axis (generalizes the gwindel SEG
  loop — lateral cantilever offset instead of twist + a tip that rides the bend).
  Mesh-sync hook gated on `_deformation.kind==='bend'` + prism token + replay-step.
- **CHEMICALLY INERT** → fleet byte-identical (baseline-diff 207↔208 = 0/35). The
  deformation is mechanical + post-growth, so the handler mutates no fluid/T; only
  the tormiq strip story gains the shear-event log line + the quartz gains a tag.
- Tenant: `tormiq_alpine_cleft` late Karakoram-Thrust shear (step 188) bends the

- Verified in the preview Three renderer (arced prism vs straight, no errors).

- **Next tenants** (same pass, new directives): calcite mechanical e-twins (lamellae

### 5.4 Syntectonic porphyroblast (snowball garnet) — OPTIONAL, the true syn-growth-stress case
- The ONE thing a grow-integrate stress field models correctly. But contested

### 5.5 Sequencing recommendation
1. **Saddle dolomite** (5.1) — growth-defect classifier + curved-face render on an

  post-growth (overprints). The category test is: *does the feature record while

  syn-growth = integrate; overprint = post-process pass.

  eye/aesthetic, don't claim a curvature law.
